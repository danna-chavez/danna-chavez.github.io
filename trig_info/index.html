Unit Circle

In algebra one you get a taste of trigonometric functions . You are probably familiar with the mnemonic “SOHCAHTOA” The sine, cosine, and tangent of an angle can be found corresponding to the sides of a right triangle. This concept is very limited for searching for angles because it only works if the angles are acute since a triangle can’t have two ninety degree angles. The concept of the right triangle is still accurate but it’s a picture that’s been zoomed. If you zoom out, you’ll see the right triangle is actually inscribed in the first quadrant of a coordinate plane. Sine and Cosine are function that turn an angle, theta, along the perimeter of a circle with the radius of one.

Think about it this way,

If you are used to seeing cosine and sine graphed as such the one to the left. I'm here to tell you that, that it is illustrating a circle. Don't believe me? I'll prove it. Pi equals 180 degrees, 3/2 pi equals 270 degrees. Both waves are creating circle with in a period of 2 pi which is equivalent to 360 degrees. Cosine and Sine functions are indeed depicting a circle.
